About Me

I am an academic researcher working at the intersection of rigorous mathematical theory, applied probability and statistics, data science frameworks, and scalable computational tools. Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Decision Sciences Department at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore (IIMB), working alongside Prof. Soudeep Deb and Prof. Anand Deo. Prior to this role, I conducted research at Sorbonne University Abu Dhabi under the supervision of Prof. Tanujit Chakraborty, focusing on pairing deep learning frameworks with real-world datasets. I completed my PhD on network-driven nonlinear epidemic model analysis and simulations with Prof. Nachiketa Mishra at the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Design and Manufacturing (IIITDM), Kancheepuram, Chennai.

My core research concentrates on developing data-driven statistical modeling for spatial scaling in Extreme Value Theory (EVT). I apply these methodologies to investigate rare event simulations, such as heatwaves and heavy rainfall. This framework allows us to model observable extreme events and leverage tail extrapolation to estimate rare event probabilities that lie well beyond the observed data range.
